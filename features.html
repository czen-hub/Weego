Limited Member Seats per City

To protect driver earnings, WeGo enforces a membership cap per metropolitan area. Once a city's active seats are filled, new drivers join a priority waitlist. When a founding member retires, they may transfer or sell their active seat to a waitlisted driver — making the membership itself a valuable asset at the end of your career, not just the beginning. The cooperative facilitates the transfer process to ensure the retiring member receives fair market value for their active seat. This prevents market saturation and keeps the earnings advantage permanent.

Cooperative Governance

You Don't Work For WeGo.
You Own It.

Every member has equal democratic power in the cooperative. No driver gets more votes for doing more rides, paying more fees, or joining earlier.

🗳️

1 Member, 1 Vote

Every driver has one vote on board elections, fee changes, and major capital decisions — regardless of tenure, ride count, or market. Tenzin's vote counts exactly the same as driver #500,000.

📋

Elected Board

The Board of Directors is elected by member-owners. Management serves at the direction of the board. No outside investors can appoint board seats or override driver decisions.

📊

Full Transparency

Annual financial reports published to all members. Every fee, every investment, every retirement trust balance — publicly available to any member who asks. No black boxes.

🔒

Pension Ring-Fence

The Retirement Trust is a legally separate entity under ERISA. The Board cannot vote to access it, invest it in the business, or pledge it as collateral. It exists solely to pay pensions.

🌐

Federated State Model

Each state operates as its own legal entity for insurance and regulatory purposes. A lawsuit in Florida cannot affect California members. The national cooperative coordinates benefits across all states.

📣

No Deactivation Algorithms

As a member-owner, you cannot be removed from the platform by an automated system. Any deactivation requires a documented human review process with the right to appeal to the board.
