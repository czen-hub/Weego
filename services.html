The "Human Premium" —
What Robots Can't Do.

Autonomous vehicles are good at point-to-point loops. They are not good at carrying groceries, steadying an elderly passenger on an icy sidewalk, or loading a wheelchair into the boot. That's where WeGo's driver-owners shine.

Assisted Mobility & Concierge

Our assisted mobility service is designed for passengers who need more than just a car — they need a capable, patient person. Our driver-owners in this tier receive additional training and earn a premium fare for the additional care they provide.

As Waymo and Tesla flood the market with AVs, WeGo deliberately positions its human drivers in the high-value, high-trust lanes that automation structurally cannot serve. This is where your income is protected against the robot transition.

The AV Fleet — Years 6–10

We Don't Fear the Robots.
We Own Them.

Every rideshare company is terrified of autonomous vehicles replacing their drivers. WeGo is the only platform where the drivers own the AV fleet — so when the robots work, the pension fund grows.

How the AV Fleet Works for You

By Year 6–8, WeGo uses its accumulated cooperative capital to purchase autonomous vehicles in bulk and assign them to the Retirement Trust. These vehicles operate 20 hours per day, netting approximately $4,000/month each after operational costs.

That $4,000 goes into the Retirement Trust — not to Uber's shareholders. When a founding driver retires after 20 years, one AV in the fleet is permanently assigned to their pension. The robot works so they don't have to.

Human drivers continue to handle the high-value, high-trust routes: assisted mobility, school transport, medical logistics, and premium corporate services. The AVs handle the 3 AM downtown loops and the routine commutes — the rides that human drivers don't want to take anyway.
