Drive & Own

Reclaiming the Road.

For too long, the drivers powering the gig economy have received the smallest share of the wealth they create. WeGo was built to permanently change that math — one driver-owner at a time.

Why WeGo Exists

In April 2026, a Gold Pro Uber driver working the SFO airport corridor ran a simple experiment: he checked the actual fare breakdown on 11 real Bay Area routes. What he found was staggering. On a $142 SFO to Napa fare, Uber kept $79 and paid the driver $63. The platform took more than the person who did the work.

On long airport routes, Uber's effective cut ranged from 50% to 58%. On some routes, the driver's per-hour earnings fell below California's minimum wage — while Uber's share stayed fixed and untouched regardless of traffic, time, or conditions.

WeGo was created to answer one question: what if the drivers owned the platform instead? Not as a protest. Not as a union negotiating for crumbs. But as a fully legal California worker cooperative where every driver is a shareholder, every decision is made democratically, and every dollar of profit returns to the people who earned it.

What Makes WeGo Different

Three principles that separate us from every other app on the market.

🏛️

Ownership, Not Employment

Every driver who pays the $500 founding fee becomes a legal member-owner of the cooperative. You have a vote on governance, a share in the profits, and a stake in the assets the cooperative builds. Uber drivers are contractors. WeGo drivers are owners.

🔒

The Sovereign Retirement Trust

The Driver's Legacy Fund is a legally walled-off trust — separate from the cooperative's operating accounts. Management cannot access it. Creditors cannot touch it. It exists for one purpose: targeting a projected ~$2,500/month pension for every driver who serves 20 years. Annual actuarial review keeps it honest.

🤖

We Own the Robots Too

Autonomous vehicles will reshape transportation in the next decade. At WeGo, the cooperative plans to purchase the AV fleet starting Year 6–8 using asset-backed hardware financing — loans secured by the vehicles themselves, not equity investment. No outside investors receive ownership; no dilution occurs. When a driver retires after 20 years, a share of that fleet generates their projected pension income. The robots don't replace the drivers. The robots work for the drivers.
