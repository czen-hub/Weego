Section 2 — The 20-Year Driver's Pension

The Driver's Legacy Fund is a pension program modeled after the military's 20-year service retirement. A driver who joins in 2026 and completes 20 years of active membership by 2046 becomes eligible for a projected ~$2,500/month for the rest of their life (the current floor target in today's dollars). The target distribution is calibrated as a percentage of AV fleet revenue — meaning as ride fares rise with inflation and the fleet grows, the pension's income base rises with it. Unlike a fixed-dollar pension that loses value over 20 years, WeGo's distribution target is designed to track economic conditions, not fight them.

The Retirement Trust is funded through two tracks that work together:

  • Track 1 — Member contributions + investment returns (the foundation). $25 of every driver's $75/month dues flows directly into the trust. At 500,000 active members, this generates $450M/year in contributions. Invested conservatively at 5% annual return, the trust reaches approximately $5.8B by Year 10 — enough to fund all early retirees entirely from investment returns, with no AV income required.
  • Track 2 — Autonomous Vehicle fleet income (the accelerator). By Year 6–8, WeGo projects owning 50,000 AVs through the Retirement Trust. A fixed portion of the 12% platform fee accumulates in a dedicated Hardware Reserve Fund from Year 1, building the down payment lenders require before asset-backed fleet financing is approved. Each AV targets $4,000–$6,000/month net income. AV revenue is the growth engine that scales pension payouts beyond what contributions alone can fund — and allows pensions to continue even if a retiree outlives contribution-driven projections.

The pension does not depend on AVs arriving on schedule. The trust is solvent from member contributions alone. AVs raise the ceiling. See Section 8 for the full AV risk analysis.

The math: A driver who starts at 25, drives for 20 years, and retires at 45 pays approximately $20,000 total in dues and fees over 20 years. At the projected ~$2,500/month target, they receive $600,000 over the next 20 years — and continuing for life. That's a projected 2,900%+ aggregate retirement benefit relative to dues paid. Projections assume current fee structures, 5% trust returns, and cooperative growth targets — actual results will vary.
Important — legal distinction: The Driver's Legacy Fund pension is a contractual benefit of cooperative membership — not a financial investment product or security. Monthly dues are cooperative membership fees; the pension is a vested benefit earned through years of service, legally analogous to a union pension or employer-sponsored retirement benefit. WeGo does not offer securities. No "return" is guaranteed; distributions are funded by cooperative operating surplus and AV fleet asset yield — not treated as a return on member deposits. Benefit amounts depend on trust fund performance and fleet income as described above.

The pension is based on 20 years of service membership, not age. If you join at 25 and complete 20 years, you are eligible at 45. The cooperative operates on the Army Model — your commitment is the clock, not your birthday.

At 20 years you receive three things immediately:

  • $20,000 equity payout — your accumulated internal capital account, paid as a lump sum on the day you complete 20 years.
  • Projected ~$2,500/month pension — begins immediately, funded from the Retirement Trust (contributions + investment returns + AV fleet income).
  • Zero platform fee on every ride — after Year 20 you keep 100% of every fare you drive. The 12% tech fee drops to $0 permanently. This benefit runs alongside your monthly pension for as long as you choose to keep driving.

If you complete fewer than 20 years, partial vesting applies: 3–5 years = 25%, 5–10 years = 50%, 10–15 years = 75%, 15–19 years = 90%. If you stop membership before 3 years, no pension vesting is earned, though your $500 membership share may be redeemable.

Section 4 — Ownership & Governance

WeGo is structured as a California Worker Cooperative Corporation under the state's cooperative laws. This means:

  • 1 Member, 1 Vote. Every member has equal voting power regardless of how long they've been a member or how many rides they do. A founder with 5 years in doesn't outvote a newer member.
  • Board elections. The Board of Directors is elected by the driver-members. Management serves at the will of the drivers, not outside investors.
  • Profit sharing. At year end, surplus revenue above operating costs is distributed back to members as patronage dividends proportional to their ride activity — not held for shareholders.
  • No outside equity. WeGo does not sell equity to venture capital firms or outside investors. The cooperative is funded entirely by member dues and revenue.

What members CAN vote on: Board composition, surplus distribution policy, new service expansions, geographic expansion priorities, annual budget approval, bylaw amendments (with supermajority thresholds for protected items).

What members CANNOT vote away (constitutionally protected for the first 10 years from founding date, then split into two tiers):

  • Standard protected (75% supermajority after Year 10): the 12% tech fee ceiling, the 1-member-1-vote rule, and the prohibition on outside equity.
  • Core endowment protected (90% supermajority, at any time): ERISA separation of the pension fund and the defined pension payout structure. Any amendment to these items can never reduce or eliminate benefits already earned by any enrolled member — the Founding 1,000's earned pension rights are permanently grandfathered and cannot be legislated away by any future membership vote, regardless of size.
Why it matters: A pure democracy can vote to raid the pension fund in a hard year. A pure board can vote to sell to Uber. The constitutional protections exist because future members who haven't joined yet — and cannot vote — deserve to inherit a cooperative that still has a pension fund and still charges 12%. You are not just voting for yourself; you are obligated to protect the members who will come after you.

Any active member in good standing — dues current, no unresolved disciplinary proceedings — is eligible to run for the Board of Directors. You do not need a business degree, a legal background, or anyone's permission. You need 20 signatures from fellow members and a one-page candidate statement.

The process:

  • Nomination window. Opens 45 days before the annual member election. Submit your candidate statement and 20 member signatures to the cooperative Secretary during this window.
  • Campaign period. 30 days. Candidates may send one cooperative-distributed message to all members and attend any member meeting. No spending limits — no campaign budgets required. The cooperative publishes all statements to its member portal.
  • Election. All active members vote. One member, one vote. The top vote-getters fill the open seats.
  • Term. 2 years. Maximum 2 consecutive terms, then a 1-year cooling-off period before running again. This prevents permanent board entrenchment.

Board composition: 7 directors total — 5 elected driver-members plus 2 independent directors appointed for regulated financial oversight (insurance, ERISA compliance). The independent directors cannot outvote the driver-elected majority on any governance issue.

Founding 1,000 priority seats: During the cooperative's first two annual elections, at least 3 of the 5 driver-member seats are reserved for founding members. This ensures the people who took the earliest risk shape the cooperative's first years — before later members, who joined with far less uncertainty, can hold a numerical majority on the board.

What the board actually does: Sets long-term cooperative strategy, approves the annual operating budget, governs geographic expansion decisions, appoints the independent fiduciaries on the Retirement Trust Board, and holds management accountable. A board member who drives the SFO corridor on Monday mornings makes different decisions than one who doesn't. That's the point.

Why it matters: Most WeGo drivers will never run for the board. But every driver knowing they could changes how management behaves. A CEO who answers to driver-elected directors answers differently than one who answers to venture capital. The founding cohort — the 1,000 drivers who took the risk before the app existed — have first claim on that accountability. You are not just an owner. You are a potential leader of this institution.

Pre-launch (before app goes live): All founding fees are held in escrow. If WeGo fails to reach its three launch milestones within 24 months of your enrollment date (CPUC license, insurance policy bound, beta app with 100+ rides), you receive a full refund of any fees paid. This protection is written into your membership agreement.

Post-launch (once the app is live in your market): If you leave before 3 years, the $500 founding fee is non-refundable — it was deployed to fund the insurance deposit, app infrastructure, and legal setup you have been benefiting from since enrollment. Partial vesting under 3 years results in no pension entitlement.

If you leave after vesting has begun (3+ years), you retain your partial pension rights at the applicable vesting percentage. Your internal capital account balance (accumulated equity credits) is redeemable at the cooperative's next annual book-value calculation — this is the formal process used by all California cooperatives to return a departing member's equity at fair value.

You cannot sell your membership share to a third party. Cooperative shares are non-transferable to non-members — they must be redeemed by the cooperative at book value. This keeps the cooperative in the hands of working drivers, not speculators or investors.

Why it matters: Two separate rules apply depending on launch status — and confusing them is how workers get misled. Pre-launch: full refund available if we don't deliver. Post-launch: your fees are invested in infrastructure, so refund policy changes. The escrow structure is the key protection — it means we cannot accept your money and disappear. Milestones must be hit first.
